The United States is a highly developed/advanced mixed economy.[43][44][45] It is the world's largest economy by nominal GDP; it is also the second largest by purchasing power parity (PPP), behind China.[46] It has the world's sixth highest per capita GDP (nominal) and the eighth highest per capita GDP (PPP) as of 2024.[10] The U.S. accounted for 26% of the global economy in 2023 in nominal terms, and about 15.5% in PPP terms.[11][47] The U.S. dollar is the currency of record most used in international transactions and is the world's reserve currency, backed by a large U.S. treasuries market, its role as the reference standard for the petrodollar system, and its linked eurodollar.[48] Several countries use it as their official currency and in others it is the de facto currency.[49] Since the end of World War II, the economy has achieved relatively steady growth, low unemployment and inflation, and rapid advances in technology.[6]

The American economy is fueled by high productivity, well developed transportation infrastructure, and extensive natural resources.[50] Americans have the sixth highest average household and employee income among OECD member states.[51] In 2021, they had the highest median household income.[52] The U.S. has one of the world's highest income inequalities among the developed countries.[53][54][55] The largest U.S. trading partners are Canada, Mexico, China, Japan, Germany, South Korea, the United Kingdom, Taiwan, India, and Vietnam.[56] The U.S. is the world's largest importer and second largest exporter.[57] It has free trade agreements with several countries, including Canada and Mexico (through the USMCA), Australia, South Korea, Israel, and several others that are in effect or under negotiation.[58] U.S. has a highly flexible labor market, where the industry adheres to a hire-and-fire policy, and job security is relatively low.[59][60] Among OECD nations, the U.S. has a highly efficient and strong social security system; social expenditure stood at roughly 30% of GDP.[8][61][7]

By 1890, the United States had overtaken the British Empire as the world's most productive economy.[62] It is the world's largest producer of petroleum and natural gas.[63] In 2016, it was the world's largest trading country[64] and second largest manufacturer, with American manufacturing making up a fifth of the global total.[65] The U.S. not only has the largest internal market for goods, but also dominates the services trade. Total U.S. trade was $4.2 trillion in 2018.[66] Of the world's 500 largest companies, 121 are headquartered in the U.S.[67] The U.S. has the world's highest number of billionaires, with total wealth of $3.0 trillion.[68][69] U.S. commercial banks had $22.9 trillion in assets in December 2022.[70] U.S. global assets under management had more than $30 trillion in assets.[71][72] During the Great Recession of 2008, the U.S. economy suffered a significant decline.[73][74] The American Reinvestment and Recovery Act was enacted by the United States Congress, and in the ensuing years the U.S. experienced the longest economic expansion on record by July 2019.[75][76][77][78]

The New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq are the world's largest stock exchanges by market capitalization and trade volume.[79][80] The U.S. has the world's largest gold reserve, with over 8,000 tonnes of gold.[81] In 2014, the U.S. economy was ranked first in international ranking on venture capital[82] and global research and development funding.[83] The U.S. spends around 3.46% of GDP on cutting-edge research and development across various sectors of the economy.[84] The U.S. has produced the world's highest number of Nobel laureates in the economics field.[85] It is also the world's fourth largest high-technology exporter.[86] The U.S. ranks second in the world by number of patent applications.[87] Consumer spending comprised 68% of the U.S. economy in 2022,[88] while its labor share of income was 44% in 2021.[89] The U.S. has the world's largest consumer market.[90] The nation's labor market has attracted immigrants from all over the world and its net migration rate is among the highest in the world.[91] The U.S. is one of the top-performing economies in studies such as the Ease of Doing Business Index, the Global Competitiveness Report, and others.[92]
